BRUNO MARS

Peter Gene Hernandez

Bruno Mars, born as Peter Gene Hernandez, is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, instrumentalist, and dancer.

Life and career:

  • 1985 - Born in Honolulu, Hawaii to Peter Hernandez and Bernadette San Pedro Bayot.
  • 1987 - His father gave him the nickname "Bruno" because of the resemblance to wrestler, Bruno Sammartino.
  • 1989 - Four year-old Bruno started to become a well-known young Elvis Presley impersonator in Hawaii with his family's band, The Love Notes
  • 1990 - He and was featured on a Hawaiian tabloid and performed in the halftime show of the 1990 Aloha Bowl.
  • 1997 - Bruno's parents got divorced leading to the disbandment of their family band. His father's businesses failed and they had to live in the "slums of Hawaii", on the back of a car, on rooftops and in an abandoned zoo.
  • 2003 - After graduating high school, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue a music career and began to adapt "Bruno Mars" as his stage name.
  • 2004 - Mars signed a record contract with Motown Records. Although no recordings were made, Motown paved a way to meet songwriter, Philip Lawrence. Mars, Lawrence and sound engineer Ari Levine produced songs under the name Smeezingtons which included famous songs such as Billionaire (Travie McCoy), Nothing on You (B.o.B), and Long Distance (Brandy). Aside from helping in producing these songs, he also starred in some of them.
  • 2010 - Bruno Mars released "Just The Way You Are" which is a single from his debut album "Doo-wops & Hooligans." The album also contained various singles that were topped the Billboard charts such as "Grenade", "The Lazy Song", "Talking To The Moon", "Marry You", and "Count on Me".
  • 2011 - Bruno won his first Grammy Award for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance for the song "Just the Way You Are" among 6 other nominations.
  • 2012 - He signed a worldwide publishing deal with BMG Chrysalis US. In the same year, he released his second album "Unorthodox Jukebox" with his single "Locked Out of Heaven." This album included record breaking songs such as " "When I Was Your Man", "Treasure", "Gorilla" and "Young Girls".
  • 2013 - Billboard has named him the Artist of the Year. It was also at the same year when his legendary Super Bowl XLVII halftime show took place.
  • 2014 - Mark Ronson starred Bruno in the song "Uptown Funk" which gained a lot of attention and became a massive success.
  • 2016 - Ronson and Mars' featured single, "Uptown Funk", won a Grammy Award for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance and Record of the Year.

    At the same year, Coldplay headlined the Super Bowl 50 halftime show, with guest performers Mars and American singer Beyonc√©. This was Bruno's second appearance at the Super Bowl halftime show. It became one of the most watched Super Bowl halftime shows in history.

    The album "24K Magic" was released with the lead single song "24K Magic". Throughout 2017 and 2018, 4 more singles from the album were released. These were "That's What I Like", "Versace on the Floor", "Chunky", and "Finesse" which were all record breaking as usual.
  • 2019 - Mars, together with Cardi B, released a single together called "Please Me" which peaked number 3 at Billboard.
  • 2021 - "Leave the Door Open" was released by Bruno Mars and American rapper Anderson .Paak under the band name Silk Sonic. The song became number 1 in New Zealand.

Awards:

Throughout Mars' career, he earned numerous major awards which includes eleven Grammy Awards, three Brit Awards, four Guinness World Records, nine American and ten Soul Train Music Awards. In addition to these awards, he also won numerous awards in a huge number of award giving bodies. The most notable ones however are:

Grammy Awards:

  • Album of the Year: 24K Magic (2017)
  • Song of the Year: That's What I Like (2017)
  • Best R&B Performance: That's What I Like (2017)
  • Best R&B Album: 24K Magic(2017)
  • Album of the Year: 25 (2016)
  • Record of the Year: Uptown Funk (2015)
  • Best Pop Duo/Group Performance: Uptown Funk (2015)
  • Best Vocal Pop Album: Unorthodox Jukebox (2013)

Brit Awards:
  • British Single: Uptown Funk (2015)
  • International Male Solo Artist (2014, 2012)
Billboard:
  • Top R&B Artist, Top R&B Male Artist, Top R&B Tour (2018)
  • Top R&B Song: That's What I Like (2018)
  • Artist of the Year (2013)
